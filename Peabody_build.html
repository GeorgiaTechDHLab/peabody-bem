Peabody Visual History

Based on work from Elizabeth Peabody, an American educator in the 19th century, this interactive tool modernizes a method of learning history chronilogically. Each square represents a year in a century, starting at 0 in the upper left hand corner and ending in 9 in the bottom right hand corner. Each year square is subdivided into 9 squares, which each have an event type. These types are shown in the table on the right. The colors in the color palette represent the countries involved in the historical timeline.

To make your own chronological history chart, take a list of events and fill in the squares accordingly. Click on the color for the country in the palette, and then fill in the square that represents the event type. Clicking on the square once makes it the solid color. To remove the color, click again. To split the colored square with another color, if the nations share the event, click again. Click again to swap the slant. Click one more time to fill the square with the new color.

Sample Event List: Chronological List of the 16th Century

  2. 1501. Henry VII. grants patent for colonizing America. To represent this event, the 6th subdivision of the square on the upper left hand is painted purple, which is the color for England.
  3. 1512. Ponce de Leon discovers Florida. Eighth subdivision of the twelfth square is brick red for Spain.
  4. 1517. Sebastian Cabot's last voyage in the English service. Eighth subdivision, purple.
  5. 1520. Vasquez de Ayllon’s Piracy on Chicora. Eighth, brick red.
  6. 1523. Verrazzani explores American coasts for France. Eighth subdivision, blue, for France.
  7. 1525. Stephen Gomez tries to discover North West Passage to India for Spain. Eighth subdivision, brick red.
  8. 1526. Pamphilo de Narvaez attempts to conquer Florida. Eighth, brick red.
  9. 1534. James Cartier discovers River St. Lawrence. Eighth, blue.
  10. 1537. Ferdinand de Soto attempts to conquer Florida. Eighth, brick red.
  11. 1540. Roberval and Cartier try in vain to colonize Canada. Eighth, blue.
  12. 1542. Ferdinand de Soto dies on the Mississippi. Ninth, brick red.
  13. 1562. Coligny sends a Huguenot Colony to America. Eighth, blue.
  14. 1565. St. Augustine, founded by the Spanish Melendez, who conquers the French colony. First and upper half of second, brick red, to represent the conquering Spaniards; also, fifth to represent the foundation of St. Augustine; and eighth, the deed of Melendez, a remarkable person. Blue in the lower half of the second, and in the third, to represent French loss.
  15. 1576. Martin Frobisher renews English Explorations. Eighth, purple.
  16. 1578. Sir Humphrey Gilbert’s Voyage and Death. Eighth, purple.
  17. 1579. Sir Francis Drake discovers Oregon, and names it New Albion. Eighth, purple.
  18. 1584. Sir Walter Raleigh gets patent to colonize. Eighth, purple.
  19. 1585. Sir W. R.’s unsuccessful colony at Roanoke, carried out by Sir Richard Grenville. Eighth, purple.
