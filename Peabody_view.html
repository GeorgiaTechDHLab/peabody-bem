Chronological List of the 16th Century

  1. 1501. Henry VII. grants patent for colonizing America. To represent this event, the 6th subdivision of the square on the upper left hand is painted purple, which is the color for England.
  2. 1512. Ponce de Leon discovers Florida. Eighth subdivision of the twelfth square is brick red for Spain.
  3. 1517. Sebastian Cabot's last voyage in the English service. Eighth subdivision, purple.
  4. 1520. Vasquez de Ayllon’s Piracy on Chicora. Eighth, brick red.
  5. 1523. Verrazzani explores American coasts for France. Eighth subdivision, blue, for France.
  6. 1525. Stephen Gomez tries to discover North West Passage to India for Spain. Eighth subdivision, brick red.
  7. 1526. Pamphilo de Narvaez attempts to conquer Florida. Eighth, brick red.
  8. 1534. James Cartier discovers River St. Lawrence. Eighth, blue.
  9. 1537. Ferdinand de Soto attempts to conquer Florida. Eighth, brick red.
  10. 1540. Roberval and Cartier try in vain to colonize Canada. Eighth, blue.
  11. 1542. Ferdinand de Soto dies on the Mississippi. Ninth, brick red.
  12. 1562. Coligny sends a Huguenot Colony to America. Eighth, blue.
  13. 1565. St. Augustine, founded by the Spanish Melendez, who conquers the French colony. First and upper half of second, brick red, to represent the conquering Spaniards; also, fifth to represent the foundation of St. Augustine; and eighth, the deed of Melendez, a remarkable person. Blue in the lower half of the second, and in the third, to represent French loss.
  14. 1576. Martin Frobisher renews English Explorations. Eighth, purple.
  15. 1578. Sir Humphrey Gilbert’s Voyage and Death. Eighth, purple.
  16. 1579. Sir Francis Drake discovers Oregon, and names it New Albion. Eighth, purple.
  17. 1584. Sir Walter Raleigh gets patent to colonize. Eighth, purple.
  18. 1585. Sir W. R.’s unsuccessful colony at Roanoke, carried out by Sir Richard Grenville. Eighth, purple.
